NVO987 Research Infrastructure

Decentralized identity, knowledge systems and machine-readable research infrastructure.

Overview

The NVO987 domain infrastructure represents an experimental framework for decentralized identity, knowledge systems, and machine-readable research environments. The project explores how identity, data provenance, scholarly publication, and automated knowledge processing can be integrated within an open web architecture.

Rather than operating as a traditional website, the NVO987 domain network functions as a distributed research infrastructure composed of several specialized nodes. Each subdomain serves a specific role within the architecture: identity management, knowledge representation, research experimentation, and public dissemination of scholarly writing.

This root domain provides a structural entry point into that infrastructure. It documents the conceptual organization of the network and links to the primary nodes responsible for identity verification, research resources, and knowledge graph services.

NVO987 Cultural Association

NVO987 – Modern and Contemporary Visual Culture is a cultural association dedicated to research, documentation, and publication in the field of modern and contemporary visual culture.

The activities of the association focus on the analysis of visual culture, the processes of image creation, and the broader media environments in which visual forms are produced and interpreted. Particular attention is given to understanding how images function within contemporary cultural systems and how visual structures evolve across different technological and historical contexts.

Research conducted within the association addresses the dynamics of visual representation, the transformation of image-based communication, and the role of visual media in shaping contemporary cultural experience. This includes investigations into the mechanisms through which images generate meaning, the structural organization of visual information, and the cultural frameworks through which visual forms are interpreted.

A significant dimension of the association’s research concerns the concept of generativity. This involves the study of processes through which images, visual systems, and symbolic forms are produced, reproduced, and transformed across technological and cultural environments. In contemporary contexts this includes algorithmic image production, generative media systems, and the interaction between human creative practice and computational technologies.

Through research activities, documentation projects, and publications, the association seeks to contribute to the interpretation of modern and contemporary visual phenomena. The objective is to support an interdisciplinary research environment connecting visual studies, media theory, digital humanities, and knowledge infrastructure.

The digital infrastructure presented on this website partially supports these activities by providing an experimental environment for decentralized identity management, machine-readable research metadata, knowledge graph development, and structured documentation of research outputs.

Decentralized Identity Infrastructure

The identity layer of the infrastructure is implemented through the decentralized identifier did:web:identity.nvo987.us. This identifier provides a cryptographically verifiable root for identity verification, public keys, signed documents, and machine-readable identity metadata.

The identity node hosts verification services, identity documents, public key directories, and linked machine-readable metadata describing the broader research infrastructure. Through this mechanism the identity node acts as a trust anchor for other services within the domain network.

Identity services and verification endpoints are available at identity.nvo987.us, where documentation, identity records, and verification mechanisms are maintained.

The complete decentralized identity document describing the cryptographic keys, verification methods, and service endpoints associated with this infrastructure can be accessed at the following location:

https://identity.nvo987.us/.well-known/did.json

This architecture follows emerging standards in decentralized identity and verifiable credentials, allowing independent systems, agents, and knowledge graphs to reference a consistent identity root.

Knowledge Systems

The knowledge layer of the infrastructure is hosted at knowledge.nvo987.us. This node focuses on structured knowledge representation, knowledge graphs, and machine-interpretable research metadata.

The objective of this component is to explore how research knowledge can be represented in formats accessible not only to human readers but also to automated agents, machine learning systems, and semantic web technologies.

Datasets, graph structures, and machine-readable schemas provide a foundation for experimental work in knowledge extraction, automated reasoning, and long-term research archiving.

The knowledge node also serves as a testing environment for structured data formats including JSON-LD, semantic graphs, and dataset catalogs.

Research Laboratory

Experimental research activities are hosted within the laboratory node at lab.nvo987.us. This environment provides a flexible space for prototyping ideas related to knowledge systems, identity infrastructure, decentralized web technologies, and automated research tooling.

The laboratory node may include prototypes, technical documentation, experimental tools, and exploratory work related to the broader NVO987 research initiative.

Unlike the identity node, which focuses on stable verification services, the laboratory environment is intentionally experimental and may evolve frequently as new ideas are tested.

The identity infrastructure also links to external scholarly identifier systems used to maintain persistent academic references and cross-platform identity records.

These identifiers provide long-term reference points across academic and knowledge infrastructures.

Research Philosophy

The NVO987 infrastructure is designed around several core principles: openness, decentralization, verifiable identity, and machine-readable knowledge.

Rather than relying on centralized platforms, the system explores how independent web nodes can cooperate through open standards such as decentralized identifiers, linked data, and semantic web technologies.

By structuring identity, data, and research outputs within interoperable web standards, the project aims to demonstrate how future knowledge infrastructures may operate in a decentralized digital environment.

Infrastructure Status

The NVO987 domain network is an evolving research infrastructure. Some components are stable and intended for long-term use, while others remain experimental as new architectural ideas are explored.

Identity services, structured metadata, and machine-readable knowledge datasets will continue to evolve as the research project develops.

Researchers, developers, and interested readers are welcome to explore the nodes of the infrastructure and follow the ongoing work through the available publications and public channels.
